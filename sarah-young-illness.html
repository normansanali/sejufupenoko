Sarah Young Illness: What Disease is She Suffering From?

July 2024 · 4 minute read

Sarah Young is a Christian author of Jesus Calling: Finding Peace in His Presence. She graduated from Wellesley College with a philosophy degree and from Covenant Theological Seminary in St. Louis with a master’s in biblical studies and counselling.

After all these years, many people are now interested in Sarah Young’s illness. What is known about Sarah Young’s disease is provided here.

From What Disease Is Sarah Young Suffering From?

Sarah was diagnosed with two infections connected to Lyme disease in 2009 and underwent “intense, expensive therapy” for six months in the US, but she did not improve. She has also had ongoing vertigo for a lot of years.

Jesus Today which continues the first-person teaching from Jesus’ structure of her previous three devotionals was motivated by these challenges. Jesus Calling, first released in 2004 and based on the author’s private notebooks, went viral and has since sold more than 5 million copies.

Since then, 6.5 million copies of it and related books have been sold including the devotionals Jesus Lives and Dear Jesus. Sarah said:

“I am convinced that if I had been healthier I would not have been able to write this book, It chronicles my journey from discouragement to hope. There seems to be a sense of hopelessness creeping into many people’s mindsets as they look at problems in their lives and in world events.”

“But the hope we can find in the Bible is a sturdy reality—no matter what is happening in our lives or in the world. My desire is that Jesus Today will be a lifeline to people who feel as if they’re sinking in hopelessness, as well as a source of encouragement to those whose lives are not so desperate.”

The Impact of ‘Jesus Calling’ Explored in Podcast

A Jesus Calling podcast focuses on the impact the devotional has had on readers’ lives through the use of personal stories. Because Jesus Calling has had a beneficial impact on his family, Phil Keaggy, a performer who has won a Dove Award from the Gospel Music Association (GMA), gave his skills and performed the background music.

At the Iowa Faith & Freedom Coalition on April 25, 2015, Wisconsin Governor Scott Walker recited passages from Jesus Calling to over a thousand attendees. “We had no idea Scott Walker had the book and would use it,” a HarperCollins representative claimed.

Hearing how ‘Jesus Calling’ affected someone’s life is always encouraging. Jesus Calling was given to female inmates at the Louisiana Correctional Institute for Women on June 27, 2017, by Louisiana First Lady Donna Edwards.

The Next Door, a nonprofit that assists women in need and offers Jesus Calling devotionals without charge to jails and prisons, gave the books. Terri Green praised Sarah Young for this devotional book and said, “It is the hottest-selling devotional book on the market anywhere.”
